üìö Medical Terms Glossary

Click to expand definitions and explanations

Progesterone

Hormone produced after ovulation that prepares the uterus for pregnancy. High levels indicate strong ovulation.

FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone)

Hormone that stimulates egg development in the ovaries. Normal levels indicate good ovarian reserve.

LH (Luteinizing Hormone)

Hormone that triggers ovulation and egg release. Essential for reproductive health.

Estradiol

Main female sex hormone controlling the menstrual cycle and reproductive system.

TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)

Hormone that regulates thyroid gland function and metabolism. Lower values often indicate better thyroid function.

Ferritin

Protein that stores iron in the body. Low levels indicate iron deficiency, which can cause fatigue and other symptoms.
